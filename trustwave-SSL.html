Trustwave SSL Certificates FAQs

Trustwave SSL Certificates are digital certificates that secure websites by encrypting data transmitted between a user's browser and the web server. They ensure that sensitive information, such as personal details and credit card numbers, is transmitted securely.

Trustwave offers various types of SSL Certificates, including Domain Validation (DV), Organization Validation (OV), and Extended Validation (EV) certificates, as well as Wildcard and Multi-Domain certificates.

: Trustwave SSL Certificates use strong encryption to protect data in transit, provide authentication to ensure users are communicating with the legitimate website, and display trust indicators like the padlock icon and "HTTPS" in the browser address bar.

DV SSL Certificates provide basic encryption and are validated by verifying domain ownership. OV SSL Certificates offer additional business authentication, while EV SSL Certificates provide the highest level of validation, including a thorough vetting process and displaying the organization's name in the browser's address bar.

Yes, Trustwave offers Multi-Domain SSL Certificates (SAN certificates) that allow you to secure multiple domain names with a single certificate.

The issuance time for Trustwave SSL Certificates varies based on the type of certificate. DV certificates can be issued within minutes, while OV and EV certificates may take longer due to the more extensive validation processes.
